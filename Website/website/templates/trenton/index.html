History of Pawns Pathways at the Plainsboro Library

In 2021, as the COVID-19 pandemic came to a close, we kicked off a special partnership with Plainsboro Library. This program was known as the Junior Chess Club. At first, we were a small group of enthusiastic volunteers with one goal in mind: to introduce children to the fascinating game of chess through fun and engaging weekly classes. As our journey went on, we started to dream bigger. We came to the realization that many children didn’t have the same opportunities as the children in Plainsboro Library, and so then and there we decided that we have to provide the world of chess to all children. This led us to become a non-profit organization known as Pawns Pathways. This allowed us to offer more classes, tournaments, scholarships, etc. Today, Pawns Pathways and Plainsboro Library together have created a unique place in Plainsboro, bringing the wonders of chess to many children.

Only the Best Education in Chess Is Good Enough

Within Plainsboro Library, Pawns Pathways has built a thriving environment for learning chess. Our thoroughly designed curriculum in combination with our experienced and passionate instructors create exciting lessons that blend chess with important life skills like critical thinking, problem-solving, and sportsmanship. This powerful combination helps us not just create good chess players, but also ambitious individuals ready to take on life's challenges. We ensure that every child comes out a better person.

Volunteers Are the Foundation of Pawns Pathways

The remarkable success story of our programs at Plainsboro Library would not be possible without the unwavering support and commitment of our extraordinary volunteers. Their profound love for chess, in combination with their dedication to community service, fosters an environment where learning thrives. Together, we are on a mission to help the community in Plainsboro Library. To ensure the best education in chess, we recruit only the most passionate individuals.

Our Exceptionally Designed Curriculum Makes Results

We realized the need for a better chess education that can create a legacy of learning, growth, and passion for chess. As an organization run by students, we know the challenges of the education system first-hand. With this in mind, we designed our curriculum ensuring every lesson we teach and every homework assignment is meaningful to the children’s chess journey. A good curriculum is nothing without the teacher that teaches it though. We ensure that every volunteer here at Pawns Pathways is able to bring out the child’s potential.

Fostering an Education in Chess via Scholarships

Our scholarships here at Pawns Pathways aim to fully unlock the potential of a child. We know that sometimes money can be the reason for not continuing a child’s chess education, but we aim to ensure that this will not be a factor. Our volunteers look at the outstanding talents of each child and their potential when teaching. There are also many mock tournaments to try and prove one’s skill. As we see the child’s dedication to the game of chess in combination with their potential, we provide them with scholarships to enter tournaments and further continue their chess education.

The Pawns Pathways Impact on the Plainsboro Community

Our partnership with Plainsboro Library is about more than just chess. We’ve seen our students grow and improve in focus, memory, and logical thinking. These benefits spill over into their school work and personal lives. Plainsboro Library is a welcoming space for chess lovers of all levels, and together, we’re building a community of lifelong learners, strategic thinkers, and passionate chess players. As Pawns Pathways, we want to leave an ever-lasting impact on the community of Plainsboro.
